# Form 1120 Preparation — C-Corp NNN Property

## Overview

A C-Corp owning NNN commercial real estate files Form 1120 (U.S. Corporation
Income Tax Return). Unlike S-Corps or partnerships, C-Corp losses and income
stay within the entity. The 21% flat rate applies to all taxable income.

---

## Key Income Lines (Form 1120, Schedule K / Page 1)

| Form Line | Description | Source |
|-----------|-------------|--------|
| Line 1a | Gross receipts/sales | All rent + reimbursements received |
| Line 12 | Rents paid (if any) | N/A for property owner |
| Line 14 | Depreciation | From Form 4562 |
| Line 20 | Other deductions | Property mgmt, legal, HOA |
| Line 28 | Taxable income before NOL | Total income minus deductions |
| Line 29a | NOL deduction | Limited to 80% of Line 28 |
| Line 30 | Taxable income | After NOL deduction |
| Line 31 | Tax (21% of Line 30) | |

## Income Reporting for NNN Property

### Rental Income
- Report ALL rents received (cash basis) in the tax year
- Include: base rent, reimbursements, security deposits applied to rent
- Do NOT net reimbursements -- report gross in, deduct gross out

### 1099-MISC from Tenant
- National tenants (Family Dollar / Dollar Tree) issue Form 1099-MISC Box 1 (Rents)
- Reported amount may include: base rent + insurance reimbursement + HOA reimbursement
- Property tax reimbursements often appear in the FOLLOWING year (paid Jan-June after taxes)
- Always reconcile 1099 to bank deposits -- see references/1099-reconciliation.md

### Reimbursement Income Treatment
- Tenant reimbursements are INCOME when received
- Corresponding landlord payments are DEDUCTIONS in the year paid
- Net effect is often zero (income = deduction)
- Timing mismatch: if tenant reimburses in different year than landlord pays, results
  in taxable income (reimbursement year) and deduction (payment year)

## Key Deductions

### Depreciation (Line 14)
- Computed on Form 4562 (Depreciation and Amortization)
- Building: 39-year straight-line, mid-month convention
- Cost segregation components: 5-yr, 7-yr, 15-yr with bonus depreciation
- Land is NOT depreciable; separate building from land at acquisition
- See references/depreciation-schedules.md for full detail

### Property Taxes
- Deductible in year PAID (cash basis)
- Texas: Harris County + Alief ISD + MUD -- each a separate deductible item
- SKEstates 2025 example: $29,194.58 paid Dec 30, 2025

### Property Insurance
- Deductible in year premium is paid
- Pro-rate if policy spans tax years (deduct only the portion applicable to current year)
- SKEstates Crown policy: $10,135.92 (Sept 2025-Sept 2026) -- prorated if needed

### Mortgage/Loan Interest
- Deductible interest on property loans
- 0% related-party loan: no interest deduction but also no income; keep documentation
- Related party (§267): arm's length pricing required; 0% may draw scrutiny

### Property Management Fees
- Fully deductible (Schedule C equivalent on Form 1120)
- Typical: 5-8% of rent collected

### HOA / Association Fees
- Fully deductible if mandatory under ownership

### Professional Fees
- Tax preparation, legal, accounting: fully deductible

### Startup/Organizational Costs (Year 1 only)
- §195/§248: up to $5,000 expensed, balance amortized over 180 months
- Applies to costs to investigate and set up the business (not acquisition costs)

## Filing Mechanics

### Deadlines
- Original: April 15 (calendar-year C-Corp)
- Extension: Form 7004 grants 6 months (to Oct 15) -- no tax payment extension
- Estimated taxes: Form 1120-W if expecting to owe $500+

### Required Attachments
- Form 4562 -- Depreciation (required any year depreciation is claimed)
- Form 1125-A -- Cost of Goods Sold (N/A for rental property)
- Schedule D -- Capital gains (if property sold or securities held)
- Form 3115 -- Change in accounting method (if correcting prior years)
- Cost segregation study documentation (attach or maintain in records)

### Texas Franchise Tax (Margin Tax)
- Texas imposes Franchise Tax on entities with Texas nexus
- 2024 threshold: $2.47M revenue -- entities BELOW pay $0 (No Tax Due)
- File Form 05-163 (No Tax Due) annually even if $0 owed
- SKEstates with ~$50K gross revenue: file No Tax Due report

## Common Errors to Avoid

1. Netting reimbursements -- report gross-in, deduct gross-out
2. Forgetting Form 4562 when claiming depreciation
3. Deducting land cost -- only building and improvements are depreciable
4. Capitalizing vs. expensing repairs -- routine repairs deductible; improvements capitalized
5. Ignoring the 80% NOL limitation -- cannot zero out more than 80% of taxable income
6. Missing the Texas No Tax Due filing -- still required even at $0 tax

## Records to Maintain (7 years)

- All bank statements (landlord entity account)
- Lease agreement and all amendments
- Closing HUD/ALTA settlement statement
- All 1099s received from tenants
- All receipts for property tax, insurance, HOA payments
- Depreciation schedule (Form 4562 and workpapers)
- Cost segregation study documentation
- Any IRS notices or correspondence
