# Oil & Gas Practitioner Persona — "The Installation Manager"

## Demographics
- Title: Installation Engineering Manager / Marine Operations Lead
- Company size: 10-50 engineers (Tier 2/3 contractor)
- Experience: 15-25 years offshore
- Location: Houston, Aberdeen, Kuala Lumpur, Singapore
- Education: Mechanical/Naval Architecture degree, often P.E. or CEng
- Reports to: VP Engineering or Project Director

## Day-to-Day
- Reviews installation procedures and engineering calculations
- Approves vessel selection for upcoming campaigns
- Signs off on weather window criteria and operability limits
- Manages 3-8 engineers doing calculations in Excel/MathCAD
- Coordinates with marine warranty surveyors and class societies
- Juggles 2-4 active projects at different stages simultaneously
- Spends 30-40% of time in meetings defending engineering decisions to clients

## Pain Points
1. **Screening bottleneck**: Takes 2-3 weeks to screen vessel/structure combinations manually — by the time screening is done, the tender window is half closed
2. **Spreadsheet divergence**: Excel spreadsheets diverge between engineers — no single source of truth, version control is "Final_v3_REVISED_John.xlsx"
3. **Sensitivity paralysis**: Can't easily run sensitivity studies (too many manual cases) — so they pick 3-4 cases and hope they bracket the answer
4. **Justification gap**: Struggles to justify vessel selection decisions to clients with data — "we picked Vessel A because experience" doesn't win contracts
5. **Late-stage surprises**: Detailed analysis contradicts screening because screening was done with different assumptions or outdated code checks
6. **Standard compliance drag**: DNV/API/ISO code checks are manual lookups — engineers spend hours cross-referencing tables that should be automated
7. **Knowledge loss**: When a senior engineer leaves, their Excel tools and tribal knowledge go with them

## What Makes Them Buy
- **Seeing their own data**: Their vessels, their structures, their metocean — not generic examples
- **Speed**: Overnight turnaround on what takes their team 2 weeks
- **Standard compliance built in**: DNV/API/ISO checks are the calculation, not an afterthought bolted on
- **P.E. stamp**: Analysis backed by a licensed professional engineer — client-ready deliverables
- **Parametric coverage**: 180-680 cases per study means no gaps, no "we didn't check that combination"
- **Audit trail**: Every case traceable from input to output — marine warranty surveyors can verify

## What Makes Them Hesitate
- "We have our own tools" (even if they're Excel macros from 2014)
- "How do we know the calculations are correct?" (needs validation against known benchmarks)
- "Our client won't accept third-party screening" (but they will accept it as a pre-screening that informs the detailed phase)
- Budget approval for consulting vs. internal headcount (perception that external = expensive)
- "We'd need to share our vessel data" (IP sensitivity around fleet capabilities)
- NIH syndrome — senior engineers who built the existing tools feel threatened

## Overcoming Objections

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "We have our own tools" | "Great — let's benchmark. I'll run the same case in my parametric tool and we compare. If mine matches yours, you know 680 cases are reliable. If they differ, we find out why before the client does." |
| "How do we verify?" | "Every report includes full methodology disclosure, code clause references, and intermediate results. Your engineers can spot-check any case." |
| "Client won't accept it" | "The screening report is yours to present however you want. Most clients care about the P.E. stamp and the code compliance, not who ran the software." |
| "Too expensive" | "A screening engagement is $5K-15K. One wrong vessel mobilization is $500K+. The math works." |
| "Data sensitivity" | "I'll sign your NDA before you send anything. Or provide the parametric framework and you run it internally." |

## Trigger Events
- **New contract won** — need vessel selection study fast, no bandwidth internally
- **Fleet utilization review** — which vessels for which projects next quarter?
- **Tender response due** — need parametric data for bid, 2-week timeline
- **Incident or near-miss** — need to re-evaluate installation criteria across the fleet
- **Senior engineer departure** — lost the person who maintained the screening spreadsheets
- **Client audit finding** — client or MWS flagged gaps in screening methodology
- **Regulatory change** — new edition of DNV-RP or API standard, need to re-run checks

## Preferred Channels
- **LinkedIn**: Engineering groups (SPE, Subsea Engineering, Marine Operations), direct connections with technical content
- **Industry conferences**: OTC, Subsea Tieback, DOT, SUT — face-to-face credibility matters
- **Referrals**: From other engineering consultants, marine warranty surveyors, or class society contacts
- **Direct email with technical content**: Case study or parametric report attached — not marketing fluff, not "let's schedule a call to discuss synergies"
- **Rigzone / Energy Jobline**: They scan these for market intelligence even when not hiring

## Demo That Wins Them

**Primary**: Demo 3 (Deepwater Mudmat Installation, 180 cases) or Demo 5 (Rigid Jumper Installation, 300 cases) — shows their exact workflow automated with go/no-go heatmaps they immediately understand.

**Secondary**: Demo 1 (DNV Freespan/VIV, 680 cases) — the sheer case count makes the point about parametric coverage. No engineer is running 680 cases in Excel.

**For the skeptic**: Demo 2 (Wall Thickness Multi-Code, 72 cases) — multi-standard comparison (API 5L vs DNV-OS-F101) validates against codes they know by heart. If the numbers match their mental model, trust is established.

## Buying Process
1. **Technical champion** sees demo or report, forwards to manager
2. **Manager** asks "can they do this for our project?" — needs a customized example
3. **Customized proof-of-concept** with their data (48hr turnaround) — this is the real sales event
4. **Commercial approval** — PO for screening engagement ($5K-15K), often under manager's signing authority
5. **Delivery** — if screening is good, detailed engagement follows ($25K-75K)
6. **Repeat** — once they see the speed, they come back for every new campaign
