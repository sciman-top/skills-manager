Five production demos have screened 1,292 cases overnight --- covering freespan VIV, deepwater installation vessel selection, pipeline wall thickness optimization, mooring system sensitivity, and cathodic protection sizing. This enables clients to compress weeks of manual engineering into hours of automated, auditable parametric sweeps.

Vamsee holds a P.E. license and is based in Houston, Texas.

## 4. Sample Past Engagement Descriptions

For "past experience" or "relevant project" fields on registration forms. These are representative descriptions --- not client-identifiable.

1. **Advised a mid-cap E&P operator on installation vessel selection for a 6-well subsea tieback in 1,800m water depth (Gulf of Mexico).** Screened 12 installation vessel configurations against structure weights ranging from 80 to 350 metric tons. Key insight: identified that a monohull construction vessel with a 600 MT crane could handle the full campaign, avoiding the cost premium of a semisubmersible crane vessel and saving an estimated $15M in vessel spread costs.

2. **Advised a private equity firm evaluating an acquisition of a pipeline integrity services company.** Provided technical due diligence on the target's freespan and VIV assessment methodology, comparing their approach against DNV-RP-F105 current practice. Key insight: the target's fatigue calculation methodology was conservative by a factor of 2--3x, meaning their reported remaining-life figures were understated --- a positive for asset valuation.

3. **Advised an EPC contractor on mooring system redesign for an FPSO redeployment from West Africa to Southeast Asia.** Reviewed the existing mooring analysis (OrcaFlex) against the new site's metocean conditions, which featured a significantly different wave-current regime. Key insight: the existing polyester taut-leg mooring was adequate for intact conditions but marginal for one-line-damaged cases; recommended upgrading two leeward lines from 160mm to 180mm polyester, avoiding a full mooring replacement.

4. **Advised a major operator's integrity team on cathodic protection remaining life for a 25-year-old subsea manifold.** Assessed anode consumption rates against original design per DNV-RP-B401, incorporating inspection data from recent ROV surveys. Key insight: remaining CP life was 3--5 years (not the 10 years assumed in the operator's integrity management plan), triggering an accelerated retrofit anode installation that prevented an unplanned production deferral.

5. **Advised a renewable energy developer on jacket foundation design feasibility for a 15 MW offshore wind turbine in 45m water depth (US East Coast).** Compared jacket vs. monopile vs. suction bucket options using parametric screening across soil conditions, wave climate, and turbine loading. Key insight: for the site's stiff clay seabed, a three-leg jacket with suction caissons offered a 20% steel weight reduction compared to a four-leg piled jacket, with significant fabrication schedule advantages.
