# OCIMF MEG4 — Mooring Equipment Guidelines

*By [ACE Engineer](https://aceengineer.com) -- Expert Offshore and Marine Engineering Consulting*

OCIMF Mooring Equipment Guidelines, 4th Edition (2018). The current industry standard for mooring equipment design, selection, operation, and maintenance.

## Key Updates from 3rd Edition

- **HMPE failure modes**: Extensively updated to address axial compression fatigue, condition monitoring, and retirement criteria
- **Snap-back zones replaced**: Entire mooring deck now treated as a danger zone — specific snap-back zone markings removed
- **Low-stiffness lines**: Preferred for terminals susceptible to waves, swell, or passing ship forces
- **Longer tails**: May be required due to longer wave periods
- **Human-centred design**: New emphasis on crew safety and equipment ergonomics
- **IMO alignment**: Addresses new SOLAS Regulation II-1/3-8 requirements

## Companion Guidance

OCIMF published "The Hazards of Snap-back" based on the Zarga incident, documenting that a 44mm sheathed UHMPE line with Euroflex tail snapped back over 15m in less than 1 second.

**Effective Mooring, 4th Edition (2019)**: Training companion for seafarers, aligned with MEG4. Addresses human factors (communication, fatigue, situational awareness).
