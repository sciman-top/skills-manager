# Prelude FLNG Mooring Failures

*By [ACE Engineer](https://aceengineer.com) -- Expert Offshore and Marine Engineering Consulting*

Shell's Prelude FLNG experienced multiple mooring system failures before its first cargo was ever loaded, demonstrating systemic design issues in side-by-side mooring systems.

## 2018 Incident (NOPSEMA 5415)

LNG carrier Gallina moored with 16 mooring lines at Prelude FLNG on 19 May 2018. Mooring line 3 found parted; all 16 lines significantly damaged.

**Root cause**: Incompatibility of nylon rope with nylon liner on FLNG fairlead. Small radius pressure points generated excessive heat and abrasion when lines stretched under tension. This was a systemic design failure, not an individual line defect.

NOPSEMA stated there could have been "complete mooring failure" with "potential for serious consequences" if it had occurred during LNG transfer.

## 2019 Steel Line Failures

Before first cargo was loaded, the facility was shut down when steel mooring lines wore faster than expected. All 16 mooring lines replaced. NOPSEMA ordered delay to startup until June 2019.

## Key Lessons

- Heat and abrasion cause nylon line failure at FLNG side-by-side operations
- Nylon rope on nylon fairlead liner is an incompatible combination
- All 16 lines damaged in a single event = systemic design failure
- Material compatibility testing must cover the full operating envelope
